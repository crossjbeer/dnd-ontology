Lore Introduction: 
	Magic doesn’t just exist; it is pulled from somewhere, like water pulled from a well.  Those capable of turning the crank may dip their bucket into the reserve, pulling from its great potential. Except, sometimes the rope just isn’t long enough. Magic’s well dips deeper than any man can go, until some time before it bubbles back again, roiling and tempestuous. 

	3000 years have passed since anyone was last able to dip into that well. In that ancient age, wizards and fae danced with liches and ghouls like leaves dance with the breeze on an autumn day. Magic suffused experience, and anyone willing to open themselves to it could feel its touch. But those days are long gone. 

Those technicolor wars and world hopping journeys that come with magic’s power are discussed only in books and bard songs now. Those artifacts which once brought fortune and awe now sit inert on display. Those dynasties forged with mystical flame stand as unwavering pillars of power. 

That is, however, a story of days gone by. This is a different story altogether. This story is yet to be written, because magic’s waters are newly rippling, and the realm will never be the same again.

History of the Province: 
It isn’t clear when the first Dwarves put down roots in the area now known as Stonegate. Written records begin several Ripplings and tens of thousands of years ago, and while they are fragmented at best, they give glimpses into a rudimentary society already on the road to modernity. Back in those days, ripplings happened with greater frequency and intensity, making the formation of a society with any longevity all the more difficult. That being said, the province is rich in archeological sites, unexplored ruins and ancient burial mounds. Artifacts sit, waiting to be discovered. The people tell family stories handed down orally over millennia. Society may be new, but community is not. 

One could look back on the subterranean people now called the Duergars. These dwarves followed Sir Grum Duergarian into the depths of Stonegate in an attempt to find a place insulated from the effects of each rippling. While successful for some time, disaster eventually struck. A period known as Abbathor’s Hammering sundered the world. Great quakes shook the earth, ripping it into pieces. This fractured the Duergarian people, casting them apart. Some groups were able to continue on, trapped beneath the earth, but thriving. Over the years, they have slowly lost much of their resemblance to their surface relatives, making them a unique adornment of the area. 

One also thinks back to ‘The Last Try’, or in dwarvish, Eth zaz yfraun. Early on, before the true chaos of the rippling was understood, a group of high dwarves convened in an attempt to end the rippling once and for all. With plans to bind the plane, the group set out, north of Moradin’s Sprue, to build their spire. This hulking object, today overgrown, sunken into the swallowed swamp and degrading, was conjured over 7 days and 7 nights, pulled from the earth, a solid spike of Harmonium, reaching nearly 700 ft into the air. Completed on the day of convergence, the closest the planes would come, the dwarves gathered their power, channeling it into their obelisk in their great attempt to control the uncontrollable. However, in the final moment, the 7 were sucked uncontrollably into the pillar of their creation, trapping their horrified expressions behind a metallic coffin. 

These stories give but a glimpse into the rich, unexplored and misunderstood history of the region of Stonegate. Currently, we move into one of the calmest periods ever afforded to the region. Wonder what will come next? 


The current state of the world: 
The Dwarf-run Province of Stonegate is bordered on the west by the ‘Glittering Hills’, an expansive, rich mountain range which runs north to south. The Gemstone Sea borders the province on the south and east. The sea is fed by a great river, Moradin’s Sprue, which runs from the Glittering hills, west to east, into the sea, bisecting the province. Most of the towns and villages of the province exist south of this river, as to the north sits The Swallowed Swamp. This remnant of The Rippled War, an expansive swathe of land magically transformed into an impassable swamp by Lord Nethergloom, reminds the citizens of Stonegate of the great power and danger lurking in each Rippling.

The capital of the region, Stonegate, sits under the Eastern coast, built into the seaside cliffs set against The Gemstone Sea. While also housing the military and political nucleus of the region, the city manages ‘The Leviathan Gate’, a massive, showy port set where Moradin’s Sprue meets the ocean. The city itself is heavily fortified, almost like they are waiting for something. 

Walking along Gold’s Vein, a main road traveling east from Stonegate, one would arrive at Berronar’s Bossom, the agricultural hub of the region. From here, one has a choice: move further east, along Granite’s Strip, and arrive at Marthommar, the learning capital of the province, or follow Pebble’s Path north to Timberhold, where Stonegate’s fine lumber is produced. 

Finally, moving deep into the Glittering Hills, to where Moradin’s Sprue turns into but a trickle from the rocks, you can find Rockwell, a large mining town, which could be better described as a series of settlements joined by minecart. There are many tiny settlements, villages, and tribes peppered across this diverse province, some named and mapped, others unknown. While mostly populated by dwarves, a vast variety of peoples and species call this place home.

Pantheon Lore: 
	The dwarven pantheon of Stonegate is led by a group of seven major deities who represent the central ideals and tensions of dwarven life. At the head is Moradin, the All-Father and god of the forge, creation, and craftsmanship—symbolized by a hammer and revered as the divine artisan who shaped the dwarves themselves. Alongside him is Berronar Truesilver, the nurturing matriarch and goddess of harvest, safety, and honesty. Clangeddin Silverbeard, the god of valor and honorable warfare, carries the imagery of an emerald-inset weapon, inspiring dwarves to fight with purpose and pride. The darker side of dwarven ambition is embodied by Abbathor, god of greed and luck, whose symbol of coins reflects his duality as both a patron of trade and a warning of corruption. Dugmaren Brightmantle, god of invention and discovery, encourages curiosity and innovation, while Deep Duerra, patron of conquest and psionics, reflects the more aggressive, subterranean instincts of the dwarves. Finally, Beryl, the stoic god of mines and light in darkness, embodies order and endurance, guiding those who delve beneath the earth.
Beyond the Core 7, the pantheon includes lesser gods and spirits that color dwarven spirituality with mystery, folklore, and regional significance. Thrain Embermantle, the god of earthquakes and revelation, is feared and worshiped by the people of Rockwell, believed to slumber restlessly within the Glittering Hills. Zardral the Whisper-Deep, deity of secrets and forgotten tongues, has cryptic followers whispered to lurk within the archives of Marthommar. Fertility, kinship, and the final rest are overseen by Garmra Stonewomb, whose stone cradle symbol blesses midwives and gravekeepers alike. Folk spirits such as The Gilded Badger, Skavun the Hollow, and Mother Emberghost weave local superstition into divine lore, blending reverence, fear, and practical faith in daily dwarven life. Finally, the enigmatic Rune-Root, a spirit tied to ancient runic magic, is venerated by secluded druids and runepriests as a living link to the old magics of a world once drenched in arcane wonder.

World Lore: 
Rippling: 
Magic comes and goes. It is not static, as is described on other planes. 
The planes dance and mingle over inhuman timescales, growing closer from time to time. 
This campaign happens at the start of a great Rippling. 
The Last Rippling: 
Happened 3000 years ago. 
The last rippling was formative in the region, giving way to an era of prosperity hitherto unknown. Cities were established in ways never before possible, thanks to the stability in the region. Trade routes gave way to inter-town commerce, and the province, as well as the surrounding lands, benefitted. 
The Rippled War was the last great conflict in the region. 
A lich, Lord Nethergloom, working in conjunction with a great darkness, conspired to merge the magic and material planes, connecting whatever dark force that lives there to the material world. 
This was fought against by The Wisps, a group of 4 prolific adventurers native to the region. They rallied troops and met Nethergloom in the fields of what is now the city of Marthommar, defeating him once and for all, or that is what they thought. 
The current Rippling: 
The planes draw near, and with it, plans set in motion. 
All across the province, individuals touched by the rippling are coming to exotic, misunderstood powers, and using them for all sorts of varied outcomes. 
However, some have prepared for this moment. 
The ‘good guys’, the dwarves of Stonegate, those oppressed and nearly vanquished by the forces of darkness all those years ago, now lie in wait, ready for their opportunity to ‘save’ everyone from that fate. 
The same darkness from before continues to pull strings from where it lies out in the ether. It still aches for our plane, as some ache for its. 
And so many free radicals whiz by.
